What Is a Residential Proxy and Why Is It Useful?

A residential proxy is an intermediary server with an IP address associated with a real
residential device instead of a datacenter. Like any other proxy server, it acts as a bridge
between the user and the Internet, concealing the user's identity and location. Since
residential devices are connected to a local Wi-FI with a genuine IP registered by an ISP,
resindential IPs are more difficult to detect by anti-bot solutions. 

In other words, a residential proxy works by redirecting online traffic generated by a user
through residential IP addresses. The advantage is that the destination server will see the
connection as coming from a reliable IP
and not from a data center server with a static IP.

In particular, the key facts to know about residential proxies are:

  • Residential IPs originate from devices such as computers and other Internet-connected
    tools, usually in a real home.
  • Residential proxy networks typically include millions of IPs, with only a subset available
    at any given time, since not all devices may be turned on.
  • As they rely on real devices, provides can guarantee broader location coverage and
    more precise targeting options.
  • Residential IPs are more legitimate and have a higher reputation compared to
    datacenter IPs.
  • They can be shared by many users simultaneously or dedicated to only one client at a
    time.
  • Residential IPs on shared proxies are generally periodically rotated by default for you.

Aspects to Consider When Evaluating Residential
Proxy Providers

When evaluating the best residential proxy providers on the market, there are certain
elements to focus on. In detail, these comparison aspects are:

  • Features: The services and products offered by the provider that enhance and complement
    its offerings.
  • Pricing: The plans available, the cost of residential proxies per GB, and the presence or
    absence of a trial and/or refund policy.
  • Proxy network: Residential IPs available, in how many countries, through what protocols,
    and support for both dedicated and shared proxies.
  • Performance: The average response time of proxy servers. Since the reported figures are
    often very different from the actual ones, it is worthwhile to analyze both.
  • Uptime: The percentage of server availability guaranteed by the provider.
  • Success rate: The average percentage of successful requests made through the proxy
    network. Again, note that the reported percentages may not match the real values.
  • Customers and partners: The number of customers that choose the provider and some of
    their best partners.
  • Customer support: The way the provider interfaces with customers, in what channels, and
    its readiness to address user issues.
  • Ethics: Whether the provider respects data privacy regulations and discourages illegal
    activities by requiring a KYC (Know Your Customer) process. Another important aspect is
    their commitment to social causes through non-profit initiatives.
  • Reliability: What users and customers think about the service they provide. A good metric
    to evaluate this characteristic is their review score on G2 and Trustpilot. Awards from G2,
    Capterra and other similar services are good indicators of reliability as well.
