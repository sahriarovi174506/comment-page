What Are Residential Proxies?

Residential proxies act as intermediaries between your device and the Internet. When
routing online traffic through them, these proxies assign you an alternative IP address
coming from a real device. The purpose of this process is to hide your IP while providing
privateness and security.

Residential proxy servers are real devices connected to home networks whose IPs have
been assigned by ISPs (Internet Service Providers), making their IPs genuine and harder to
detect. This also means that providers offer the ability to select proxy servers in specific
countries and even cities.

How a Residential Proxy Server Works

A residential proxy server is a physical device that allows external users to use its IP
address. In detail, the process of routing traffic through residential proxies involves the
following steps:

  1. The configured residential proxy intercepts the online request made by the user.
  2. The proxy forwards the original request to the destination server.
  3. The server elaborates the request, which carries the IP of the residential proxy, and
    sends the response to the proxy.
  4. The proxy forwards the server response back to the user, completing the request cycle.

Note that during this process, the user's IP and identity have never been exposed to the
destination server.

Why Use Residential Proxies

The top three reasons to use residential proxies are:

  • Anonymity and enhanced online privacy: Residential proxies add an extra layer of
    privacy and anonymity by replacing your IP with that of a legitimate device in a home
    network. This strengthens security, protects against tracking, and ensures a
    confidential online experience.
  • Reduced risk of IP bans and blacklisting: These proxies use IPs associated with real
    residential devices. As a result, the reputation of residential IPs is high, which reduces
    the risk of experiencing blocks and bans. That minimizes the likelihood of being
    flagged or restricted by sites, even when using automated bots. Plus, even if you get
    blocked, rotating residential proxies will give you a new IP in minutes.
  • Access to geo-restricted content anywhere and everywhere: By redirecting Internet
    traffic through devices in specific regions, you can unlock access to content that would
    otherwise be geographically restricted in your country. This means that residential
    proxies are a great way to overcome government restrictions, expanding the reach of
    available information and entertainment.

Types of Residential Proxies

Depending on the protocol used by the proxy and the nature of their IP, resident proxies can
be classified into different types. Time to see them all!

Note: The categories below are not mutually exclusive. So, you can have a dedicated static
HTTP residential proxy as well as a shared rotating SOCKS residential proxy.

HTTP/HTTPS/SOCKS

Residential proxy servers can operate on different protocols. Generally, the best providers
offer HTTP, HTTPS, and SOCKS residential proxies. Each performs better in different
scenarios, supporting specific online activities.

Dedicated

Dedicated residential proxies allocate unique IP addresses exclusively to individual users.
That mechanism ensures a persistent, reliable, and stable connection with the same IP,
improving security and stability. These proxies are also called “private proxies.”

Shared

Shared residential proxies distribute IP addresses among multiple users. This means that
several users can access the same IP at the same time. While these proxies are a cost-
effective solution, they can have higher latency and be less reliable than dedicated proxies.
At the same time, they provide access to a lot of different IPs.

Static

Static residential proxies assign users a fixed IP address over time. This type of proxy can
be both shared and dedicated, and they are also referred to as ISP proxies.

Rotating

Rotating residential proxies have a pool of IPs available and continue to switch the exit IP
between them. This dynamic approach improves anonymity and reduces the risk of IP
blocking during activities such as web scraping. This is the most common type of
residential proxy server.
